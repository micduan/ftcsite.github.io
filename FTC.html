Free The Children is a non-profit organization that believes that children are the key in order to eliminate poverty in the future. Free The Children works with students of all ages in order to engage them and motivate them to make a difference in the world. With the adopt a village program, they aim to remove barriers that prevent people from attending school with the goal to end the cycle of poverty.

When Craig Keilburger was 12 years old, he was reading the Toronto Star where he learned about a boy named Iqbal. Iqbal lived in South Asia and was sold into slavery at the age of four. He spent 6 years making carpets in a factory. When he escaped, he went around the continent informing others of the horrors he went through. At 12 years old, Iqbal was murdered for speaking out.

Free The Children currently works in more than 45 countries and works with more than 2.3 million youth in their innovative education and development programs.

A subdivision of the program, Adopt A Village, encourages children all around the world to reach their full potential by giving them the rights to things which all humans deserve to have. Click here to learn more about the problem and see what we are working towards.
